The problem

Every enrollment requires a physical appointment at the guardhouse

At most estates, golf clubs, and business parks today, enrolling someone into the Hikvision facial recognition system still means a physical meeting at the guardhouse or security office. Every time. No exceptions.

New resident or member fills in a paper form or PDF.
They make an appointment with the security person responsible for enrollment.
Both must physically meet at the guardhouse, same time, same place.
Security person manually types the details into the Hikvision system on the guardhouse computer.
A photo is taken right there using the Hikvision device.
Person is enrolled and they test it at the gate.

This requires a physical appointment, both parties present, 15 to 20 minutes per person, the guardhouse computer to be accessible, and one person at a time. It becomes a total bottleneck. It is impossible on weekends or holidays unless someone staffs the guardhouse.

For a site going live with 2,000 people: 2,000 individual appointments. Roughly 500 hours of work.

That is operational drag before the system starts delivering value.

Some sites have improved this slightly with digital forms sent via WhatsApp, but even those still require heavy manual work per person: downloading photos, copy-pasting data, assigning access levels, and checking whether the person is actually allowed access. Neither approach scales beyond a handful of people per week.

Works with HikCentral

Your Hikvision setup stays exactly as it is

HikCentral already has a self-registration feature: someone scans a QR code, uploads a photo from their phone, and the access control device itself validates that the photo is good enough to recognise the person at the gate. If the device accepts the photo, it will work. That is a strong guarantee.

OnboardAI builds on top of this. It does not bypass or replace the device validation. The flow is additive: OnboardAI handles the smart onboarding steps that HikCentral cannot do, then hands the approved enrollment to HikCentral, which does what it is already good at. Two layers of validation. Neither replaces the other.

How it works

Walk through the full workflow

The onboarding form

Members receive a link via WhatsApp or email. When they open it on their phone, they see a clean 5-step wizard designed for all ages, including older residents who may not be comfortable with complex apps. Large text, simple language, one thing per screen.

The five steps are Welcome, Details, Contact, Photo, and Terms. The AI validates the photo, checking for a clear face, correct framing, and minimum resolution before the form can be submitted. If the photo fails validation, the member is shown exactly what to fix and prompted to try again.

The admin dashboard

The administrator sees a live enrollment queue. A stats strip at the top shows pending submissions, enrollments today, flagged issues, total active members, and the on-site agent connection status. The queue is colour-coded: green for AI-verified submissions that are ready to approve, amber for submissions needing manual review, red for issues that need attention.

Admins can approve or reject individual submissions, or bulk-approve all AI-verified ones in a single click. No manual data entry is required at any point.

Architecture

A layer on top, not a replacement

OnboardAI sits above your existing HikCentral installation. It runs on a central server, cloud-hosted or on-site at the security office or main operations office. A lightweight on-site agent creates the bridge between OnboardAI and HikCentral, communicating over HTTPS with no inbound firewall rules or VPN required.

Your existing Hikvision cameras, readers, and HikCentral licenses are unchanged. OnboardAI simply adds the onboarding intelligence on top and pushes approved enrollments through when they are ready.

The HikCentral API integration is in active testing, leveraging the existing self-registration pipeline that HikCentral provides.

Getting started

Five steps from setup to first enrollment

1

Deploy OnboardAI on a server

Provision a server, either a cloud VPS or an on-site machine in the site office. OnboardAI is installed and running in under 15 minutes.

2

Configure the access control connection

Install the lightweight on-site agent on a machine with network access to your access control system. Paste in the connection details from the admin panel. This is a once-off setup with your IT team.

3

Import your member list

Upload a CSV with your current member or resident list. OnboardAI uses this to validate new enrollments and match them against known people.

4

Share the onboarding link

Generate an enrollment link from the settings panel. Share it via WhatsApp broadcast, email, or print the QR code for reception. Members can start enrolling immediately.

5

Review and approve from the dashboard

Log in to the admin dashboard to see incoming enrollments. AI-verified submissions can be bulk-approved in one click, then queued for automatic transfer to the access control system.
